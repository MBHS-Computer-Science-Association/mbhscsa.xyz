About the club

Are you interested, passionate in coding, programming, or making things on computers in general? Want to expand your horizons and get ready for computer science classes in high school?

The McKinney Boyd High School Computer Science Club is currently looking to expand our outreach to middle and elementary schoolers. Our current priority is working with students in schools that feed into Boyd. We'll bring our practical insight into CS from high school to passionate future coders.

Our first focus is establishing a Computer Science Club at Dowell Middle School, one of four middle schools feeding into Boyd. Of incoming students from middle schools, a plurality of students are from DMS.

So congratulations, lucky Lions! We're coming to your school! Please share this link to everybody you know, especially those interested in programming or coding.

Coming into existence in the 2016–2017 school year, the Dowell Middle School Computer Science Club will be sponsored by the Club Outreach initiative of McKinney Boyd High School Computer Science Club. Membership in the club will be free but requires attendance after school at our meeting times. Exact dates will be decided as next school year approaches.

What do we do?

We will learn how to code and discuss the cool stuff we learn. Some of the things you can do in our meetings:

  • Learn coding
  • Discuss interesting things you've made
  • Think about computer science concepts
